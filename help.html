Your Consumer Rights

Last reviewed: February 2026

üõ°Ô∏è Under NCC General Consumer Code of Practice (¬ß5.3), every Nigerian mobile subscriber has the legal right to dispute inaccurate billing ‚Äî and to receive a full investigation and written response within 30 days.

What the law says

The Nigerian Communications Commission's General Consumer Code of Practice for Licensees mandates that all telco service providers must: maintain accurate billing records, provide itemised billing on request, investigate consumer complaints within stipulated timeframes, and offer refunds or credits for proven over-billing.

Key rights you hold

  • Right to accurate billing: You may only be charged for data you actually used and authorised.
  • Right to dispute: You can formally dispute any bill you believe is inaccurate, at any time.
  • Right to evidence: Your telco must provide raw usage records if requested during a dispute.
  • Right to investigation: The NCC must investigate your complaint if the telco fails to resolve it within 30 days.
  • Right to refund: Proven over-billing must be corrected with a full refund or equivalent credit.

How AI Data Meter enforces these rights

AI Data Meter's forensic PDF dispute report is structured specifically to satisfy NCC ¬ß5.3 requirements. It includes your device ID, measurement methodology, byte-level evidence, anomaly logs, and a formal legal citation block ‚Äî everything a regulator needs to open a case against a telco.

NCC ¬ß5.3 Filing Guide

Updated: February 2026

Step 1: Generate your forensic report

From your dashboard, navigate to Dispute Cases and click Generate PDF. AI Data Meter will produce a fully formatted forensic audit report with your measured data, the telco's reported figures, the variance calculation, and a legal citation of NCC ¬ß5.3.

Step 2: File with the NCC

AI Data Meter can submit your dispute directly to the NCC portal via our Dispute-Forge engine. Alternatively, you can manually submit at ncc.gov.ng/consumers or call the NCC toll-free line: 0800 CALL NCC (0800 2255 622).

Step 3: Track your case

Your dispute will receive an NCC reference number. AI Data Meter tracks your case status and notifies you of updates. The NCC must respond within 30 days under its own code of practice.

What happens if the telco disputes your evidence?

Your AI Data Meter report uses OS-level byte measurements ‚Äî the same measurement point your device's billing API reports. Our methodology documentation is available to support your case. The variance threshold we use (5%) is deliberately conservative and accounts for legitimate protocol overhead.

üìã Important: Keep your telco SMS/USSD reports (the ones that say "You've used X MB") as these form the D_telco side of the variance calculation. AI Data Meter stores these automatically if you forward them through our app.

Privacy Policy

Effective: January 2026

What we collect

AI Data Meter collects only the minimum data required to provide our service: packet metadata (destination IP address, byte count, app UID, timestamp, and screen state). We never collect message content, browsing history, or any personally identifiable payload data.

Where data is stored

All raw monitoring data is stored locally on your device in an AES-256 encrypted database. Only aggregated audit reports ‚Äî generated explicitly by you for dispute purposes ‚Äî are transmitted to our servers.

Who can access your data

Only you. Our server-side logs are encrypted and access-controlled. We do not share, sell, or transfer your data to any third party, including advertisers or telcos. We will comply with lawful Nigerian court orders only.

Data retention

Local monitoring data is kept for 90 days by default (configurable). Server-side dispute records are retained for 3 years to support ongoing NCC cases. You can request deletion at any time via Settings ‚Üí Data & Privacy.

Data Handling Charter

Effective: January 2026

üîí AI Data Meter processes network metadata only. We never read, store, or transmit packet content, message text, call audio, or browsing URLs.

Data minimisation principle

Every data point we collect has a specific, documented purpose. Destination IPs are used to identify app categories. Byte counts form the D_device value. App UIDs identify which application generated traffic. Screen state identifies phantom drain during idle periods.

Encryption standards

Local database: AES-256 with device-bound key derived via Android Keystore / iOS Secure Enclave. Transmission: TLS 1.3 with certificate pinning. Server storage: AES-256 with hardware security module key management.
